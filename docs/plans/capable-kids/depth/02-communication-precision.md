# Depth Pack 02 — Communication Precision

**Kid:** Mit (grade 5, 10–11) · **Dose:** 10–15 min/day, 5 days/week · **Cycle:** 12 weeks
**Promise:** by week 12 Mit can explain something he learned for 3 minutes — structured, low-filler,
watchable on a recording he scored himself — and has spoken in front of a real audience.

The phone camera is the coach here, not the parent. Recording → self-score → re-record is the
feedback loop that makes this self-improving; the parent's job is audience and celebration.

## 3. Ladder L0→L5

| Level | Milestone (objective, checkable) |
|---|---|
| L0 | Baseline recorded: one describe-and-draw scored, one 1-min retell recorded and kept (never delete the baseline — it's the week-12 payoff) |
| L1 | Describe-and-draw **≥80%** on 5-shape figures; says the 5 story elements from memory |
| L2 | 1-min retell hits **all 5 elements**, self-scored from the recording ≥8/10 |
| L3 | 3-min teach-back **≥16/20** on rubric; **<5 fillers/min** (down from baseline) |
| L4 | Family demo night delivered start to finish; volunteers for a class presentation |
| L5 | Attends a Gavel Club / YLP session and delivers a prepared 2-min speech to non-family |

## 4. Daily dose protocol (by phase)

**Weeks 1–2 — Baseline + description precision**
- Day 1: record baselines (describe-and-draw score + 1-min retell of any movie/book). Lock the file away.
- Days 2–5, 10 min: describe-and-draw, alternating roles. Mit describes a hidden figure
  (start: 3 shapes; add one shape each success) — parent/sibling draws ONLY what the words say.
  Score = elements drawn correctly / total. Then swap roles once — drawing from dad's description
  teaches what good description sounds like.
- Rule: the describer may not use hands, may not look at the drawing until done.

**Weeks 3–6 — Structured retell**
- Learn the frame as a hand: thumb = who/where, index = problem, middle = what they tried,
  ring = how it ended, pinky = zinger (final sentence that lands).
- Mon/Wed/Fri, 10 min: pick anything (today's recess drama, a chapter, a YouTube video he watched),
  record a 1-minute retell on the phone, watch it back, self-score /10 (2 pts per element),
  re-record ONCE if he wants. Twice is not allowed — perfectionism is the enemy of reps.
- Tue/Thu: describe-and-draw continues (keep the precision muscle warm).

**Weeks 7–12 — Teach-it-back + mechanics**
- Mon/Wed: 3-min "teach dad something from school this week" — recorded. This IS retrieval practice;
  it counts as a Pack 01 school-content day too.
- Fri: watch Monday's recording together, score the rubric, pick ONE mechanic to fix next week
  (never two). Filler-counter game during dinner: everyone counts everyone's "um/like/so" —
  parent included. Mit charts his fillers/min.
- Teach-back rubric (/20): hook that earns attention (0–4) · clear structure — first/then/because (0–4) ·
  one concrete example or demo (0–4) · pace with actual pauses (0–4) · ending that lands, no trail-off (0–4)

## 5. Parent playbook

**Sunday session (15 min):** record the numbers → one specific celebration ("that pause before the
answer — that's a pro move") → set one micro-goal → real wins into /journey/admin → /trophies.

**Mid-week (2+ hr/week budget):**
- Be the drawer, be the audience, be the taught. Ask one honest follow-up question per teach-back —
  being asked a real question is the strongest signal that the explanation mattered.
- Model it: do YOUR own 1-min retell of your workday once a week using the 5-finger frame, badly.
  Let him score you. (Him catching your missing "zinger" is worth ten lessons.)

**In-the-moment lines:**
- Hates watching his own recording (everyone does): "everyone hates their own voice — pros included.
  Score the structure, not the voice."
- Freezes mid-retell: silence, let him find it. If he asks for help: "look at your fingers."
- Never correct grammar or content mid-recording. Notes wait for the rubric.

## 7. Performance outlet

- **Week 6:** family demo night (shared with Pack 01) — one retell, live.
- **Week 8–10:** volunteer for anything at school that involves standing up front.
- **Week 12:** visit a Gavel Club or YLP session ([find via local Toastmasters district](https://d4tm.org/ylp-gc);
  online youth-run clubs exist for ages 10–18). Prepared 2-min speech. The visit itself goes on the
  brag sheet regardless of how the speech goes.
- **Week 12 ritual:** watch the L0 baseline recording next to the week-12 recording, together.
  That before/after IS the trophy — screenshot both, put the pair in /journey/admin.
