# 14-Day Diverse Vegetarian Meal Plan (No Eggs)

**For:** 40-year-old male, 150 lb (68 kg), Charlotte NC, lifting 3x/week
**Daily targets:** ~2,000-2,200 kcal · ~90-110g protein · 35-40g fiber
**Philosophy:** Maximum diversity — nuts, seeds, dals, beans, soy, dairy, whole grains, roots/tubers, greens, vegetables, fruits, salads. Nothing repeats more than twice in 14 days.

## How to Use

- **Whey:** 1 scoop (24g protein, ~120 kcal) daily in milk
- **Soak** nuts, dals, beans overnight; soak chia/sabja before eating (never dry)
- **Pair iron + vitamin C** at every lunch (lemon, tomato, bell pepper, amla)
- **Tea/coffee between meals**, not with food (protects iron absorption)
- **Brazil nuts: 1 nut, 2-3x per WEEK only** (selenium toxicity risk if daily); see Specialty Nuts section below
- **Specialty nuts** (pecans, hazelnuts, macadamia, pine, chironji, tiger nuts): rotate per the Specialty Nuts table — the more nutrient-dense, the less often
- Quantities are starting points — adjust to appetite. When appetite is low, prioritize liquids (whey, milk, buttermilk) and soft foods (yogurt, dal).

## 14-Day Diversity Summary

**Roots & tubers rotated:** Sweet potato, beetroot, carrot, turnip, parsnip, rutabaga, radish, potato (sparingly). These deliver fiber, potassium, beta-carotene, nitrates (good for blood pressure and training), and prebiotics for gut health.

**Nuts rotated:** Almonds, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, pecans, hazelnuts, macadamia, pine nuts (chilgoza), chironji, tiger nuts, peanuts, Brazil nut (2x only)
**Seeds rotated:** Chia, flax, pumpkin, sunflower
**Dals/pulses rotated:** Moong, masoor, toor, chana, urad, panchmel, rajma, lobia, kala chana, matki
**Soy rotated:** Tofu, paneer (dairy), tempeh, soya chunks, cottage cheese
**Grains rotated:** Brown rice, jowar, bajra, ragi, quinoa, oats, dalia
**Greens rotated:** Spinach, methi, kale, Swiss chard, broccoli
**Fruits rotated:** Banana, apple, orange, papaya, guava

## Specialty Nuts — Quantity & Rotation

**Principle:** the more concentrated a nutrient, the less often you eat it. Base nuts (almonds, walnuts) can stay daily; these specialty nuts rotate in small amounts.

| Nut | Key nutrient | Serving | How often | Caution |
|-----|-------------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Brazil nut | Selenium (very high) | 1 nut | **2-3x per week only** | One nut ≈ 100% daily selenium; daily intake risks toxicity |
| Pecans | Antioxidants, manganese | 5-6 halves (~20g) | 3-4x per week | Calorie-dense, portion only |
| Hazelnuts | Vitamin E, folate, magnesium | 8-10 nuts (~20g) | 3-4x per week | No toxicity concern |
| Macadamia | Monounsaturated fat | 4-5 nuts (~15g) | 2-3x per week | Highest-calorie nut |
| Pine nuts (chilgoza) | Vitamin K, zinc, iron | 1 tbsp (~14g) | 2-3x per week | Expensive, calorie-dense |
| Chironji (charoli) | Protein, calcium, fats | 1 tbsp (~10g) | 2-3x per week | Use as garnish amount |
| Tiger nuts | Prebiotic fiber, resistant starch | 20-30g | 3-4x per week | A tuber, not a nut; soak first; high fiber = gas if overdone |

**Sample weekly nut rotation (slot into the Early AM / mid-morning seed slot):**
| Day | Nut |
|-----|-----|
| Mon | Almonds (base) |
| Tue | Walnuts + 1 Brazil nut |
| Wed | Pecans |
| Thu | Hazelnuts |
| Fri | Almonds + tiger nuts |
| Sat | Macadamia |
| Sun | Pine nuts + chironji |

Repeat next week, swapping in 1 Brazil nut on a different day so it lands 2-3x/week total. No specialty nut appears more than 3-4x/week.

## Low-Appetite Adjustment

On days eating feels hard, prioritize in this order: whey shake → buttermilk/yogurt → dal → everything else. The liquid and soft items alone (whey + milk + yogurt + buttermilk) give ~45g protein with minimal effort. Don't force the rest — build back up as appetite returns.
